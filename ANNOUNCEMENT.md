## Why ALGOL 26?

ALGOL 26 is not a general-purpose language. It is **the language of AGI**.

Built on ALGOL 68's rigorous foundation, ALGOL 26 adds what AGI demands:

- **Dependent & refinement types** — encode AI invariants directly in types
- **Probabilistic programming** — native `prob` expressions for uncertainty
- **Causal modeling** — `causal` blocks for reasoning about interventions
- **Formal verification** — `verify` annotations for provable correctness
- **Ownership & borrowing** — memory safety without GC (critical for real-time AI)
- **Neuro-symbolic fusion** — blend neural networks and symbolic reasoning seamlessly
- **Hardware acceleration** — GPU kernels as first-class constructs
- **Concurrency primitives** — actors, async/await, channels built-in

This is **the language that thinks**. And we're writing it **in the open**.

## Why Nexus?

AGI cannot be centralized. It cannot be controlled by a single company or government.

**Nexus** provides:
- **Decentralized storage** (IPFS + Solid) — user-owned data
- **Secure communication** (Gibberlink) — P2P encrypted channels
- **Distributed compute** — federated learning across the network
- **Transparent governance** — community-driven decision-making

AGI built on Nexus belongs to everyone. Training happens across thousands of nodes. No single point of failure. No corporate gatekeeping.
